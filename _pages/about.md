## Research & Publications

### A Cross-Modal Adaptive Alignment and Sparse Retrieval Framework for Social Media Popularity Prediction  
*First Author* | Under review (AAAI 2027)  
We tackle the challenge of fine-grained cross‑modal interaction and semantic loss in user‑agnostic popularity prediction. The proposed framework introduces learnable dual‑view prompts to capture global and local semantics, and uses a three‑way loss for joint classification and regression. A sample‑selection strategy combined with GBDT two‑stage fusion reduces feature dimensions by 30% and improves training efficiency by 30%. On the SMPD‑Image dataset (486k samples), our method achieves a Spearman correlation of 0.523 and reduces MAE by 15% compared to the benchmark.
